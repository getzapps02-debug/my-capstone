## Confirmed Decisions

### 2026-06-15 - Working Mode

- Use the coaching path.
- Elicit consequential UX and visual decisions before writing the final spines.

### 2026-06-15 - Experience Direction

- The experience should feel calm and evidence-first.
- Lead with a guided investigation rather than a general analytics dashboard.
- Primary sequence: reconcile data, choose a Shortfall, review Contributors, inspect evidence, replay events.
- Keep charts, Transactions, Contributor evidence, and Replay synchronized.
- Explain rankings and calculations in plain language.
- Clearly distinguish imported facts, user-provided information, calculations, derived findings, and uncertainty.
- Refuse to rank Contributors when Minimum Evidence is not met.
- Keep graph evidence secondary and expandable.
- Optimize first for desktop web, where charts and evidence can appear side by side.
- Emotional outcome: "I understand what happened, and I can verify it."

### 2026-06-15 - Workspace Structure

- Use persistent left navigation on the primary desktop surface.
- `Investigate` is the default guided workspace.
- `Accounts & Data` owns imports, Transactions, and reconciliation.
- `Investigations` provides access to prior Investigation results.
- `Settings & Privacy` owns export, local-data controls, and deletion.
- The Investigation uses a visible step indicator while preserving context in one workspace.

### 2026-06-15 - Investigation Workspace Layout

- Use a three-region desktop Investigation workspace.
- The top region contains the Account Balance chart, Safety Threshold, period controls, and Investigation progress.
- The main-left region contains ranked Contributors with plain-language summaries.
- The right region is a persistent evidence inspector for source Transactions, calculations, limitations, and optional graph relationships.
- Selecting a Contributor or evidence item synchronizes all relevant regions without navigating away.
- Replay mode emphasizes the chronological timeline while preserving the selected Contributor and synchronized evidence context.

### 2026-06-15 - Visual Posture

- Use a quiet analytical visual posture.
- Inherit the base component vocabulary and accessibility behavior from shadcn/ui, with a restrained brand-layer customization.
- Use neutral light surfaces and restrained blue as the primary accent.
- Use amber for uncertainty, incomplete evidence, and reconciliation warnings.
- Reserve red for destructive actions and genuine errors.
- Use sans-serif typography with tabular numerals for monetary values, dates, rankings, and calculations.
- Use crisp, lightly rounded panels, subtle borders, and restrained elevation.
- Maintain moderate information density with expandable detail.
- Do not use gradients, decorative financial imagery, or crime-board/detective visual metaphors.

### 2026-06-15 - Evidence Disclosure

- Use progressive verification rather than displaying all evidence at once.
- Contributor rows initially show rank, monetary impact, comparison when available, timing, and a one-sentence non-causal explanation.
- Selecting a Contributor opens full evidence in the persistent right inspector.
- Source Transactions are immediately visible in the inspector.
- Detailed calculations are available in a collapsed section by default.
- Graph relationships appear only under `Related evidence` when they clarify the finding.
- Limitations and uncertainty remain visibly present and are not hidden in tooltips.
- Imported facts, user-provided Context, calculations, and derived findings use explicit provenance labels.

### 2026-06-15 - Responsive Scope

- Desktop at 1024px and wider uses the full three-region Investigation workspace.
- Tablet from 768px through 1023px places the chart above tabbed `Contributors` and `Evidence` regions.
- Phone below 768px supports reviewing data, Contributors, and evidence.
- CSV mapping and Transaction Replay remain accessible on phone but display a `Best on a larger screen` notice.
- No required functionality becomes inaccessible at smaller widths.
- Dense tables transform into stacked rows when horizontal comparison is not essential.
- Persistent desktop navigation becomes a drawer below the desktop breakpoint.

## Inherited Constraints

- Local single-user browser application for current Chrome or Edge on Windows 11.
- Core workflow sends no financial data or telemetry outside the local environment.
- WCAG 2.2 AA target; keyboard-operable core journey.
- Charts require text summaries and tabular alternatives.
- Replay respects reduced-motion preferences.
- Product language must not claim causation or provide financial advice.
- First-time evaluators should reach a supported explanation from Sample Data within five minutes.
